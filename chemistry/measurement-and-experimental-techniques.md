## Qualitative VS Quantitative

* Qualitative
  * Deals with description
  * Data is to be observed, not measured
* Quantitative
  * Deals with numbers
  * Data is to be measured

## Accuracy VS precision

* Accuracy is how close to the true value the data is
* Precision is how close the values are to each other

## Presentation of calculations

* Use decimals, not fractions
* 3 significant figures
* Include units

## Presentation of diagrams

* Draw and label
* Outline only (DON'T SKETCH)
* Correct proportions
* Cross section/ side view
* Use sharp pencils

## Apparatus

### Mass

* Electronic balance
  * Measures in grams (g)
  * More percise and faster compared to beam balance
  * Remember to zero (tare) the balance before taking readings
  * Take readings to the msot apporapriate decimal place
  * Accuracy of 0.01g

### Time

* Digital stopwatch
  * Rest the stopwatch before using it to take time
  * Take readings to the nearest second
  * Account for human reaction time (0.1- 0.3 s)

### Temperature

* Laboratory thermometer
  * Usual range is between -10Â°C and 110Â°C
  * Filled with mercury
  * Take reasings at the level of the thermometer to avoid parallax error
  * Take readings to 0.5Â°C if each division on the scale corresponds to 0.5Â°C

### Volume of liquid

* Beaker
  * Used to estimate the volume of the liquid only
  * Normalls is used to mix, hold or heat liquids only
* Measuring cylinger
  * Take readings to 0.5$$cm^3$$
  * More accurate than beaker
  * Each divison on the scale corresponds to 1$$cm^3$$
  * Take readings on the menisucs to avoid parallax error
* Burette
  * Take readings up to 0.05$$cm^3$$
  * Each divison on the scale corresponds to 0.1$$cm^3$$
  * More accurate than beaker and measuring cylinder
  * Delivers liquid drop by drop
  * Take readings on the meniscus to avoid parallax error
* Pipette
  * Take accurate readings of a fixed volume of liquids
  * Take readings to 1 decimal place
  * Take readings on the meniscus to avoid parallax error

### Volume of gas

* Gas syringe
  * Take readings to the nearest $$cm^3$$
  * Measure up to 100$$cm^3$$ of gas

### Others
